ULTRA SIMPLE DIET PLAN
=======================

Goal: ~2000 calories/day, ~160g protein
At your size and activity level, this creates roughly a 200-400 cal/day
deficit to start. As walking and exercise increase, the deficit grows.
Expect roughly 0.5-1 lb/week of loss. Slower than crash diets but
sustainable and you keep your muscle.


THE RULES
----------
1. Eat a palm-sized portion of protein at every meal.
2. Fill half your plate with vegetables.
3. Keep starches to a fist-sized portion.
4. Drink water, black coffee, or plain tea. Nothing else.
5. If you're hungry between meals, eat more protein or vegetables.
6. Carry a water bottle. Aim to fill and finish it 3-4 times per day
   (~100-120 oz total). High protein requires adequate water.


DAILY TEMPLATE
===============

BREAKFAST (~500 cal, ~40g protein)
  Pick one:
  - 3-4 eggs scrambled + toast
  - Greek yogurt (plain, full fat) + handful of berries
  - Protein shake (2 scoops whey) + banana
  - Oatmeal made with milk + 2 eggs on the side

LUNCH (~600 cal, ~50g protein)
  Pick one:
  - Grilled chicken breast + rice + steamed vegetables
  - Turkey and cheese sandwich on whole wheat + side salad
  - Large salad with grilled chicken or salmon + dressing on the side
  - Deli meat roll-ups with cheese + fruit + handful of nuts

DINNER (~700 cal, ~50g protein)
  Pick one:
  - Baked chicken thighs + roasted vegetables + small potato
  - Ground turkey stir fry + rice
  - Salmon or fish fillet + steamed broccoli + sweet potato
  - Lean beef patty (no bun) + big salad + side of beans

SNACK IF NEEDED (~200 cal, ~20g protein)
  Pick one:
  - Greek yogurt
  - Protein shake
  - String cheese + turkey slices
  - Cottage cheese
  - Hard boiled eggs (2)


RAMPING UP PROTEIN
====================
If you are not used to eating this much protein, do not jump to 160g
on day one. Your digestion needs time to adjust.

  Week 1:   Add protein to each meal. Don't worry about hitting the target.
  Week 2:   Aim for ~120g/day. Add a protein shake if meals fall short.
  Week 3+:  Work toward the full ~160g/day.

If you get bloated or constipated, you increased too fast. Drop back
and increase more slowly. Drink more water and eat more vegetables
(fiber helps — see below).


CALIBRATING YOUR PORTIONS
============================
You do not need to count calories forever. But you probably don't know
what 2000 calories looks like yet.

Do this: Track everything you eat in a free app (MyFitnessPal or similar)
for 5 days. Just log honestly — no changes needed. This shows you:
  - Where your calories are actually coming from
  - How much protein you're really getting
  - Which meals are bigger than you thought

After 5 days, you will have a much better eye for portions. Then you
can stop tracking and use the palm/fist system for the long run.
Repeat for a few days anytime you feel progress has stalled.


ANTI-INFLAMMATORY EATING
==========================
With chronic sciatica and hip issues, what you eat affects your pain
levels. The overall pattern of this diet (protein + vegetables + whole
foods) is already anti-inflammatory. To push it further:

Prioritize:
  - Fatty fish 2-3x per week (salmon, sardines, mackerel) — omega-3s
    directly reduce inflammatory markers
  - Colorful vegetables and berries (antioxidants)
  - Olive oil as your primary cooking fat
  - Nuts, especially walnuts and almonds
  - Turmeric and ginger (add to cooking, stir fries, or tea)

Reduce:
  - Fried foods and seed oils (pro-inflammatory)
  - Processed meats as a daily staple (occasional is fine)
  - Excess sugar (drives systemic inflammation)
  - Highly processed packaged snacks

Consider:
  - Fish oil supplement (2-3g combined EPA/DHA per day) if you don't eat
    fish regularly. Talk to your doctor first if you take blood thinners.


FIBER — DON'T IGNORE THIS
============================
High protein + low fiber = constipation. At 57, gut health matters.

You probably don't need to track fiber if you follow the "half your plate
is vegetables" rule. But be aware:
  - Target: 25-30g fiber per day
  - Best sources already in this plan: broccoli, berries, beans, oats,
    sweet potatoes, mixed greens
  - If things get backed up: add a daily serving of beans/lentils or
    a fiber supplement (psyllium husk)
  - Increase fiber gradually, same as protein. Too fast = gas and bloating.
  - More fiber requires more water.


GROCERY LIST (WEEKLY STAPLES)
==============================

Protein:
  - Chicken breast or thighs
  - Ground turkey
  - Eggs (2 dozen)
  - Greek yogurt (plain)
  - Whey protein powder
  - Deli turkey
  - Salmon or fish fillets (fresh or frozen) — aim for 2-3x/week
  - Cheese (sliced or string)

Vegetables:
  - Broccoli
  - Spinach or mixed greens
  - Bell peppers
  - Onions
  - Frozen stir fry vegetables (easy backup)

Starches (small portions):
  - Rice
  - Potatoes or sweet potatoes
  - Whole wheat bread
  - Oats

Other:
  - Bananas
  - Berries (fresh or frozen)
  - Nuts (almonds, walnuts, or mixed)
  - Olive oil
  - Turmeric, ginger, garlic powder, salt, pepper
  - Beans or lentils (canned is fine)


WHAT TO CUT
=============
- Soda, juice, sweet tea, energy drinks
- Alcohol (or limit to 1-2 per week max — see note below)
- Fast food
- Chips, cookies, candy as daily habits
- Creamy sauces and heavy dressings (use mustard, hot sauce, vinegar)

A note on alcohol: If you use alcohol to take the edge off anxiety, be
honest about that. Alcohol increases anxiety the next day (rebound effect),
disrupts sleep, and adds empty calories. It feels like it helps in the
moment but makes everything harder. The CBT guide and meditation plan
address the anxiety directly. If cutting back feels impossible, mention
it to your doctor — there are effective, low-stigma options for help.


KEEP IT SIMPLE
===============
- Hit your protein, eat vegetables, keep portions reasonable.
  That does 90% of the work.
- Meal prep helps. Cook protein in bulk on Sunday. Rice keeps all week.
- Eating the same meals repeatedly is fine. Boring works.
- One bad meal does not ruin a week. Just eat normally at the next meal.
- If you struggle with food choices because of anxiety or emotions,
  the CBT guide is more important than the specific foods on this list.
  Fix the relationship with food first. The details follow.
