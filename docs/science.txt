PROTEIN
Recommendation: 30g minimum per meal, 130-150g daily for adults 50+ during weight loss
Evidence: STRONG

Muscle Protein Synthesis Threshold
Morton et al: 0.4 g/kg/meal optimally stimulates MPS
Maximal MPS: 0.24-0.4 g/kg body weight (19-32g for 80kg)
Higher end for older adults (anabolic resistance)
PMC: PMC7285146

Even Distribution Benefits
Crossover study (8 adults): Even distribution (31.5g breakfast, 29.9g lunch, 32.7g dinner) > skewed distribution (10.7g, 16.0g, 63.4g)
PMC: PMC4018950

Weight Loss and Muscle Preservation
Protein >1.3 g/kg/day prevents muscle mass decline during weight loss
91kg × 1.3-1.6g/kg = 118-146g daily (130-150g within range)
Clinical Nutrition ESPEN, 2024

Breakfast Protein in Older Adults
RCT: 30g protein at breakfast (vs dinner) = greater muscle mass gains
40 elderly women, placebo-controlled, double-blind
PMC: PMC8724572

Total Daily Protein During Weight Loss
Meta-analysis: Protein >1.3g/kg/day prevents muscle mass decline
Clinical Nutrition ESPEN, S2405-4577(24)00176-1

VITAMIN D3
Recommendation: 2000 IU daily
Evidence: STRONG

Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guidelines
Recommends 1,500-2,000 IU daily for adults
Higher than IOM's 600-800 IU RDA

2024 Narrative Review
2000 IU daily raises and maintains serum 25(OH)D >50 nmol/L in >99% of adults
Maintains >75 nmol/L in >90% of adults
PubMed: 38337676

Safety Profile
No safety concerns with 2000 IU for several years
Safe even with sufficient baseline vitamin D
PMC: PMC10053989

Effectiveness Comparison
2000 IU maintains levels longer than 1000 IU
MDPI Life Sciences, 2023

2025 Evidence-Based Review
2000 IU/day prevents diseases and deaths
MDPI Nutrients, 2025

CALCIUM
Recommendation: 500mg calcium citrate daily (1000mg total with diet)
Evidence: STRONG

RDA for Men 51-70
Men 19-70: 1000mg calcium daily
Men 71+: 1200mg daily
NIH ODS Fact Sheet

Fracture Risk Reduction
Meta-analysis: 500-1200 mg/day calcium + 400-800 IU/day vitamin D
Total fractures reduced 15%, hip fractures reduced 30%
PMC: PMC2621390

Combined Calcium and Vitamin D
~30% fracture risk reduction with ~1000 mg/day elemental calcium
Vitamin D enhances calcium absorption
Mayo Clinic

Weight Loss and Bone Health
Caloric restriction increases bone loss risk
Calcium supplementation preserves bone mineral density
Critical for adults 50+ during weight loss

MAGNESIUM
Recommendation: 200-400mg magnesium glycinate before bed
Evidence: MODERATE (mixed but reasonable)

Sleep Quality RCT
250mg magnesium bisglycinate + 1523mg glycine
RCT showed improvements vs placebo: sleep duration, deep sleep, efficiency
PMC: PMC12412596

Mechanism of Action
Binds to GABA receptors, activates GABA
Reduces nervous system excitability
Glycine interacts with NMDA receptors, promotes relaxation
Lowers core body temperature for deeper sleep

Muscle Relaxation
Inhibits N-methyl-d-aspartate receptor
Promotes muscle relaxation via suppression of intracellular calcium

Dosage Guidelines
Standard adult: 200-400mg elemental magnesium
Starting dose: 100-200mg at night
Most adults stay at or under 350mg/day from supplements

Mixed Evidence Note
Some RCTs show benefit, others minimal effect
Benefits more pronounced with low baseline magnesium intake
"Often marketed for sleep/mood but not proven in all human studies"

OMEGA-3
Recommendation: 1-2g EPA+DHA daily
Evidence: STRONG (with safety caveat)

American Heart Association
AHA recommends 1-2g EPA+DHA daily for secondary CVD prevention
Evidence-based safe and effective range

Cardiovascular Benefits
Reduces cardiovascular mortality, non-fatal MI, CHD events
Higher EPA+DHA intake linked to lower CVD risk
PMC: PMC12628397

Dose-Response Effects
3-4g reduces triglycerides 20-50% (TG >150 mg/dL)
<2g/day significantly decreased total cholesterol and LDL-C
>2g/day cholesterol benefits attenuate or reverse
PubMed: 32951855

CRITICAL SAFETY - Atrial Fibrillation Risk
RCTs show >2g associated with increased AF risk
Protective effect at moderate amounts (up to ~750 mg/day)
Doses >2g increase atrial fibrillation risk

Anti-Inflammatory Effects
EPA and DHA modulate inflammation, platelet function, autonomic function
Substrates for specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs)
Benefits for inflammatory conditions including back pain

Back Pain and Sciatica
RCT: Omega-3 significantly reduced low back pain
550mg EPA + 205mg DHA daily for 3 months
Mendelian randomization: causal association between omega-3 and reduced LBP risk
"Safer alternative to NSAIDs for nonsurgical neck/back pain"
PubMed: 16531187

Evidence Quality Note
Some moderate/high-quality evidence suggests little effect on mortality/CVD
More recent meta-analyses show benefits for specific outcomes (MI)
2025 systematic review: "current clinical evidence remains inconclusive"
Conflicting evidence across studies

ANTI-INFLAMMATORY DIET FOR SCIATICA
Recommendation: Fatty fish 2-3x/week, extra virgin olive oil, colorful vegetables
Evidence: MODERATE

Omega-3 and Back Pain
RCT: 15 mL fish oil daily (550mg EPA, 205mg DHA) for 3 months
36 women aged 18-22: marked reduction in low back pain

Mendelian Randomization Study
Causal association between genetically increased plasma omega-3
Reduced risk of low back pain in European population
Frontiers in Nutrition, 2022

Omega-3 vs NSAIDs
Safer alternative to NSAIDs for nonsurgical neck/back pain
Reduces inflammatory processes with benign side effect profile
PubMed: 16531187

Intervertebral Disc Degeneration
Omega-3 reduces IVD degeneration
EPA and DHA are substrates for SPMs
SPMs resolve acute inflammation and pain
PMC: PMC6929565

Extra Virgin Olive Oil
Rich in oleocanthal: NSAID-like anti-inflammatory effects
Monounsaturated oils with anti-inflammatory properties

Important Limitation
2025 systematic review: "Current clinical evidence regarding omega-3 efficacy in pain management remains inconclusive"
Some RCTs show benefit, others minimal effect
More research needed
Frontiers in Medicine, 2025

EGG CONSUMPTION
Recommendation: 7-14 eggs/week for healthy adults; 4 eggs/week for those with heart disease or high cholesterol
Evidence: MODERATE

American Heart Association Guidelines (2019)
Healthy adults: up to 1 whole egg daily (7/week)
Healthy older adults with normal cholesterol: up to 2 eggs daily (14/week)
People with heart disease or high cholesterol: limit to 4 yolks/week
Cleveland Clinic

Umbrella Review (January 2025)
Higher egg intake weakly associated with increased LDL cholesterol (7.39 mg/dL) and total cholesterol (9.12 mg/dL)
Also associated with improved HDL cholesterol (1.37 mg/dL)
No evidence of association with cardiovascular outcomes or all-cause mortality
"No sufficient quality of evidence to discourage egg consumption"
Very low quality of evidence overall
ScienceDirect: S0939-4753(25)00003-1

Randomized Controlled Trial (2025)
Daily consumption of 2 whole eggs (14/week) for 8 weeks in adults with hyperlipidemia
Following DASH diet, egg consumption did not negatively affect endothelial function or LDL cholesterol
Journal of the American Nutrition Association

Observational Study (2025)
Consuming eggs 1-6 times/week associated with 29% lower risk of CVD-related death in healthy older adults
Even among participants with dyslipidemia: 27% lower risk of CVD-related death
Monash University study

Meta-Analysis Findings
Meta-analysis of 28 RCTs: high egg consumption increased total cholesterol, LDL, and HDL, but not LDL-to-HDL or TC-to-HDL ratios
Meta-analysis of 8 RCTs: >4 eggs/week vs <4 eggs/week showed no association with blood lipid profile or blood pressure differences

Conflicting Evidence Note
Earlier systematic reviews found greater egg consumption associated with increased CVD mortality risk (HR 1.09 per 50g daily)
Recent evidence more permissive, especially when eggs consumed as part of healthy dietary pattern
Substantial heterogeneity and generally low-quality evidence across studies

EVIDENCE QUALITY SUMMARY

STRONG (multiple RCTs and meta-analyses)
Protein: 30g/meal, 130-150g daily for adults 50+ during weight loss
Vitamin D3: 2000 IU daily for optimal serum levels
Calcium: 1000mg total daily (500mg supplement + 500mg diet) for men 51-70
Omega-3: 1-2g EPA+DHA daily (AHA guideline, AF risk >2g)

MODERATE (some RCTs support, mixed or limited evidence)
Magnesium glycinate: 200-400mg for sleep/muscle (benefits in low-baseline individuals)
Anti-inflammatory diet: Fatty fish, olive oil, vegetables for sciatica (some RCTs positive, 2025 meta-analysis inconclusive)
Egg consumption: 7-14/week for healthy adults (AHA 2019, heterogeneous evidence, recent RCTs permissive)

CORRECTIONS MADE
Omega-3 reduced from 2-3g to 1-2g (AF risk >2g)
Added atrial fibrillation risk warning
Updated protein targets from 120-140g to 130-150g
Added egg consumption limits: 7-14/week for healthy adults (AHA 2019)
