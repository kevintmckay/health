GOAL
7-8 hours quality sleep on consistent schedule
Sleep affects pain sensitivity, weight loss, recovery, motivation

PICK YOUR TIMES
Choose wake time, work backward 8 hours, stick to these 7 days/week
Examples:
  Early bird: 10:00 PM bedtime → 6:00 AM wake
  Standard: 10:30 PM bedtime → 6:30 AM wake
  Night owl: 11:00 PM bedtime → 7:00 AM wake

Most important: Keep wake time same every day, including weekends
Anchors circadian rhythm faster than anything else

WEEK 1-2: SET THE ANCHOR
Pick wake time, set alarm, get up at that time no matter what
Get outside within 30 min of waking, 10 min daylight resets internal clock (overcast days still work)
Stop caffeine after 12 PM noon (6-8 hour half-life, 3 PM coffee still in system at 11 PM)
No naps >20 min, if must nap before 2 PM only

WEEK 3-4: BUILD WIND-DOWN
30-60 min before bed:
  Dim lights (overhead off, lamps on)
  Screens off or night mode
  No work email, news, stressful content

15-30 min before bed:
  Light stretching or McGill Big 3 low intensity (optional)
  Read book (paper, not screen)
  Warm shower/bath (cooling effect afterward promotes sleep)

At bedtime:
  Lights out
  Phone face down or another room
  If not asleep in 20 min, get up, sit quietly in dim light until drowsy, return to bed

BEDROOM SETUP

Temperature: 65-68°F ideal
  Cool room + warm blanket best
  Fan helps temperature and white noise

Darkness: As dark as possible
  Blackout curtains if street light
  Cover LED lights (alarm clock, charger)
  Sleep mask if blackout curtains not option

Noise: Quiet or consistent background (fan, white noise machine)
  Earplugs if noisy environment
  Phone on silent (not vibrate)

Bed: Use for sleep only (not TV, phone scrolling, work)
  Read in chair, not bed
  Move to bed only when drowsy
  Trains brain bed = sleep

SCIATICA AND SLEEP POSITIONS

On back (best for most):
  Pillow under knees
  Flattens lumbar curve, takes pressure off spine

On side:
  Pillow between knees
  Keep knees slightly bent
  Keeps hips aligned, prevents rotation

Avoid: Stomach sleeping (forces spinal extension and neck rotation), twisted positions

THINGS THAT WRECK SLEEP
Alcohol: feels like helps but fragments sleep, kills deep sleep stages (if drink, stop 3 hours before bed)
Large meals within 2 hours bedtime (acid reflux, discomfort)
Exercise within 2 hours bedtime (raises core temperature)
Checking clock when wake at night (creates anxiety, turn clock away)
Blue light from screens after wind-down starts
Irregular schedule (sleeping in weekends undoes whole week)

TROUBLESHOOTING

Can't fall asleep:
  Get up after 20 min, sit in dim light, return when drowsy
  Don't lie there frustrated (trains brain bed = awake)
  Try body scan: mentally relax toes to head, one body part at a time

Wake in middle of night:
  Normal, most wake briefly 2-3x/night
  Don't check phone or clock
  If still awake after 20 min, get up briefly then return

Wake too early:
  Within 30 min of alarm: just get up
  Hours early: check if going to bed too early

Still exhausted after 8 hours:
  Could be sleep apnea (very common at this weight and age)
  Symptoms: loud snoring, gasping at night, dry mouth on waking, morning headaches
  Talk to doctor, sleep study can diagnose
  Untreated sleep apnea sabotages everything else

PROGRESS MARKERS
Week 1: Consistent wake time established
Week 2: Caffeine cutoff and morning light are habits
Week 3-4: Wind-down routine in place, falling asleep faster
Week 6+: Waking before alarm naturally
Week 8+: More energy, less pain sensitivity, better workouts
