ANXIETY-EATING CYCLE
Trigger → Anxious thought → Uncomfortable feeling → Urge to eat → Eat (temporary relief) → Guilt → Repeat
Goal: Break chain between feeling and eating

STEP 1: FOOD/MOOD LOG (WEEKS 1-2)
Every time you eat outside planned meals, write:
Time
What I ate
Hunger? (physical or emotional)
Feeling (anxious, bored, stressed, tired, frustrated, lonely, in pain)
Trigger (what happened 30 min before)
After (how do I feel now)

STEP 2: CATCH THE THOUGHT (WEEKS 3-4)
Common automatic thoughts:
"I need something to take the edge off"
"I've had a hard day, I deserve this"
"One won't hurt"
"I'll start fresh tomorrow"
"I can't relax without eating something"
"Screw it, what's the point"

When urge hits: "What thought just went through my head?"
Write it down

STEP 3: CHALLENGE THE THOUGHT (WEEKS 5-6)
Questions:
Is this actually true, or just feels true?
What would I tell a friend who said this?
Will eating actually solve what I'm anxious about?
How will I feel 30 minutes after eating this?
What feeling am I actually trying to fix?

Reframe:
Old: "I need something to take the edge off"
New: "I'm anxious. Food won't fix it. Feeling will be gone in 20 min whether I eat or not"

Old: "I deserve this after today"
New: "I deserve to feel good tomorrow morning. Eating won't help that"

Old: "Screw it, what's the point"
New: "That's frustration talking, not logic. One good choice right now matters"

STEP 4: URGE SURFING (WEEKS 7+)
Urges peak and pass in 10-20 minutes
1. Notice: "I'm having an urge. That's fine. It's just a feeling"
2. Rate it: 1-10
3. Set timer 15 minutes
4. Do instead: walk outside, drink water, breathing exercise, call someone, small task
5. Rate urge again after timer
6. If still want to eat after 15 min + honest reflection, eat slowly and intentionally

STEP 5: ALTERNATIVE RESPONSES
Match replacement to actual need:
Comfort: warm shower, tea, call friend
Distraction: walk, podcast, puzzle, small project
Numbing: sit with feeling using meditation
Reward: non-food rewards (music, show, 20 min hobby)
Boredom: check what you're actually feeling (often anxiety)

THINKING TRAPS
All-or-nothing: "I ate a cookie so day is ruined" → Reality: one cookie is 200 calories, day is fine
Catastrophizing: "I'll never change" → Reality: you're working on it right now
Emotional reasoning: "I feel like a failure, so I am" → Reality: feelings are not facts
Should statements: "I should be able to just stop" → Reality: decades of habit don't vanish overnight
Permission giving: "I've been good all week" → Reality: not punishment/reward system, it's lifestyle

PRACTICE SCHEDULE
Week 1-2: Food/mood log only, just observe
Week 3-4: Catch automatic thoughts, write them down
Week 5-6: Challenge and reframe thoughts
Week 7-8: Practice urge surfing, delay 15 min
Week 9+: Build alternative response toolkit
Ongoing: Daily meditation

ALCOHOL AND ANXIETY
Alcohol temporarily reduces anxiety but causes rebound anxiety next day
Creates cycle: Anxiety → drink → relief → worse anxiety → drink again
Also: destroys sleep, 150-300+ cal/drink, lowers inhibitions around food, worsens inflammation
Use same CBT tools: catch thought, challenge it, ride wave, 4-7-8 breathing
If can't cut back despite wanting to, tell doctor

REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS
Goal: eat emotionally less often, not never
Progress: 5x/week → 3x/week → 1x/week → occasionally (massive win)
Setbacks are data, not failures
Be patient, rewiring decades of coping patterns
