SEVERITY LEVELS

MILD: Increased back tightness or dull ache, no radiating leg pain, can still function
MODERATE: Radiating pain into glute/leg, walking uncomfortable, sitting long makes worse
SEVERE: Sharp constant radiating pain, difficulty walking/standing, numbness/tingling in foot, significant limitation

MILD FLARE
Keep: Walking (reduce 5-10 min, flat ground), McGill Big 3 (check form), hip stretches (piriformis may help), resistance exercises (skip chair squats if aggravate), meditation, diet, sleep, CBT logging
Add: Standing back extensions 3-4x/day (hands on hips, lean back, hold 5 sec, repeat 5x), ice 15 min after activity, extra piriformis/hip flexor stretching 2-3x/day
Duration: 2-5 days usually

MODERATE FLARE
Keep: Walking (shorten to 5-10 min or gentle house movement), McGill Big 3 (only if no increase radiating pain), piriformis/hip flexor stretch (gently), glute bridges (often tolerable), meditation (pain increases anxiety increases pain), diet, sleep, CBT logging
Pause: Resistance exercises (wall push-ups, chair squats, band rows), any exercise increasing radiating pain
Add: Standing back extensions 5 reps, 5-6x/day, alternate ice (15 min) and heat (15 min) on lower back, change positions frequently (don't sit >20-30 min), walk gently 2-3 min every 30 min sitting, OTC anti-inflammatory per label if tolerated
Duration: 1-2 weeks usually, see doctor if not improving after 1 week

SEVERE FLARE
Keep: Meditation (need it most), diet (don't let pain excuse emotional eating, use CBT tools), sleep schedule (pain disrupts sleep, hygiene matters more), CBT logging ("pain is trigger for eating"), hydration
Pause: Walking beyond basic daily movement, all exercises except gentle piriformis if tolerated
Add: Standing back extensions if tolerated (try cautiously), lying on back with calves on chair (90-90 position) 10-15 min several x/day to unload spine, position changes every 20 min, OTC anti-inflammatory per label
See doctor if: Pain not improving after 2 weeks, numbness/weakness in foot/lower leg, difficulty controlling bladder/bowels (EMERGENCY - ER immediately, cauda equina syndrome), pain so severe can't sleep despite medication
Duration: 2-4 weeks, medical guidance recommended

RESUMING AFTER FLARE

Flare <1 week:
Resume where left off but at previous week's intensity
Example: were on week 6 → resume at week 5 levels
Return to current week within 3-5 days if feeling good

Flare 1-2 weeks:
Drop back 2 weeks in all progressions
Rebuild over 1-2 weeks

Flare >2 weeks:
Drop back 3-4 weeks
Rebuild gradually, don't rush
Consider PT before resuming

General rules:
Start with walking before adding exercises
Add McGill Big 3 and hip stretches next
Add resistance exercises last
If any exercise brings back radiating symptoms, stop, wait few days before retry
Progress should feel easy at first (intentional)

PREVENTING FUTURE FLARES
Consistent McGill Big 3 (core endurance protects spine)
Strong glutes (glute bridges/clamshells take load off spine)
Flexible hip flexors and piriformis (reduces nerve compression)
Lower body weight (less spinal compression)
Good sleep (reduces pain sensitivity)
Movement throughout day (don't sit hours without moving)
Managing stress/anxiety (tension increases muscle guarding around spine, compresses nerves)

WHAT NOT TO DO
Don't stop everything and lie in bed for days (prolonged rest makes sciatica worse after first 24-48 hours)
Don't push through sharp radiating pain
Don't abandon diet ("nothing matters anyway" = all-or-nothing thinking trap)
Don't restart entire plan from scratch (haven't lost progress, just need brief rebuild)
Don't try to stretch or massage pain away aggressively
Don't ignore severe symptoms (numbness, weakness, bladder issues)
