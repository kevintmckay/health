WALKING
Most studied intervention for deconditioned adults, first-line sciatica treatment
Promotes disc nutrition, reduces nerve irritation
Start: whatever duration manageable without triggering sciatica (even 10 min)
Add 2-5 min/week
Target: 30-45 min most days
Flat, even surfaces to start
See walking-plan.txt for 12-week progression

MEDITERRANEAN DIET FOR WEIGHT LOSS
6ft/235lbs: losing 20-30lbs dramatically reduces spinal load and sciatica
~120-134g protein/day (minimum 26g/meal for muscle preservation)
Mediterranean framework: fatty fish 2-3x/week, EVOO as primary fat, whole grains daily
Legumes 3-4x/week, colorful vegetables, fruit 2-3 servings/day
Fresh herbs liberally, fermented dairy (yogurt, small amounts feta)
Adults 50+ need 25-30g minimum/meal to maximize muscle protein synthesis
Red meat no more than 2x/month, poultry 2-3x/week
Cut liquid calories (soda, juice, alcohol)
Anti-inflammatory foods reduce pain and support recovery
Track food 5 days at start to calibrate portions
See quick-reference.txt

Core (McGill Big 3): curl-up, side plank, bird-dog
  Builds endurance around spine, protects against sciatica
Hip: glute bridges, clamshells, hip flexor stretch, piriformis stretch
  Addresses hip pain and sciatica contributors
  Hip flexor tightness and glute weakness common drivers
Resistance: wall push-ups, chair squats, resistance band rows
  Preserves muscle during weight loss (critical at 57)
  Without resistance training, significant weight lost will be muscle
See core-exercises.txt

LONG-TERM VIEW
Months 3-6:
  Walking habit 30-45 min, 5-6x/week
  Progress exercise variations (counter push-ups, deeper squats, heavier bands)
  Weight loss continues, reassess calories if stalls
  Consider light dumbbell work if spine/hips tolerate

Months 6-12:
  Explore new activities (hiking, cycling, gym, yoga)
  Resistance training progress to full gym program if desired
  Reassess with doctor: bloodwork, sciatica status, medication review
  Weight approaching goal, shift to maintenance calories
  Habits are lifestyle, not "program"

Beyond 12 months:
  Maintain what built
  Goal shifts to strength, mobility, quality of life
  Annual checkups with doctor

FLARE-UP PROTOCOL
See flare-up-protocol.txt
Quick reference:
  KEEP: meditation, diet, hydration
  MODIFY: walking (shorten/pause), exercises (stop if aggravate)
  RESUME: gradually, drop back 1-2 weeks in progression
  SEE DOCTOR: flare >2 weeks, numbness/weakness in foot/leg, bladder/bowel issues (emergency)

IMPORTANT NOTES
Get medical clearance before starting
Consider 2-3 PT sessions for specific sciatica triggers and personalized modifications
Sharp/radiating pain = stop signal, not push through
Track weight weekly (same time, same conditions)
Tell one person, accountability improves adherence
