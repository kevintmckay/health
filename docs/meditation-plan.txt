WHY MEDITATION HELPS
Reduces baseline anxiety (not just in moment)
Improves ability to notice urges without acting
Lowers cortisol (stress hormone driving belly fat storage)
Reduces pain perception (relevant to sciatica)
Builds pause between feeling and reaction (exact skill for emotional eating)

Don't need to clear mind or be good at it
Just sit and practice noticing

BREATHING AWARENESS TECHNIQUE
1. Sit in chair or lie on back
2. Close eyes or look at spot on floor
3. Breathe normally, don't force
4. Pay attention to air coming in and going out
5. When mind wanders (constantly), notice and bring back to breathing
6. Noticing and returning IS the exercise, not failure, it's a rep

6-WEEK PROGRESSION

WEEK 1: 3 MINUTES
Once/day, set timer, sit quietly, focus on breathing
3 min feeling hard is normal, do it anyway

WEEK 2: 5 MINUTES
Once/day, sit, breathe, notice when mind wanders, return
Spending most time distracted is fine

WEEK 3: 5 MIN + BODY SCAN
2 min breathing
Slowly scan from feet to head
At each area, notice what feel (tension, warmth, nothing)
Don't try to fix, just notice
Builds skill of observing discomfort without reacting (same as urge surfing)

WEEK 4: 7 MINUTES
3 min breathing + 4 min body scan
May start noticing anxiety as physical sensation (tight chest, clenched jaw, shallow breathing)
Progress - getting better at catching early

WEEK 5: 10 MINUTES
3 min breathing + 5 min body scan + 2 min open awareness
Open awareness: stop focusing on anything, let sounds/thoughts/sensations come and go
Watch like cars passing on road, you're observer not traffic

WEEK 6+: 10-15 MINUTES
Find mix that works
Some days all breathing, some body scan, some just sitting
Habit matters more than technique

WHEN TO MEDITATE
Best times (pick one, be consistent):
  Morning right after waking (before phone, before coffee)
  After walk
  During pre-bed wind-down

If only one: morning (sets baseline anxiety lower all day, fewer eating triggers)

ANXIETY EMERGENCY TOOL: 4-7-8 BREATHING
Not meditation, rapid calm-down for when anxiety spikes
1. Breathe in through nose 4 sec
2. Hold 7 sec
3. Breathe out slowly through mouth 8 sec
4. Repeat 3-4 times
Activates parasympathetic nervous system, slows heart rate, lowers adrenaline
Use before opening fridge

COMMON OBSTACLES

"I can't stop my thoughts"
Not supposed to, practice is noticing and returning
Meditation full of distractions where you kept returning = successful

"I feel more anxious when sit still"
Normal first 1-2 weeks, not creating anxiety, noticing anxiety always there but covered by distractions
Gets better, if overwhelming open eyes and shorten session

"I keep forgetting"
Anchor to something already do: after brushing teeth, after morning coffee poured, after walk
Same time, same place, every day

"I fell asleep"
Sit upright instead of lying down

"I missed day/week/month"
Sit 3 min today, you're back
