DIET PLAN vs. MEDITERRANEAN DIET: SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE COMPARISON
Updated: 2026-04-01

================================================================
1. CARDIOVASCULAR DISEASE
================================================================

Mediterranean (STRONG evidence):
- PREDIMED RCT (n=7,447): 28-30% CVD risk reduction
  HR 0.70 (95% CI: 0.54-0.92) for EVOO arm
  Stopped early for benefit
  Source: Estruch et al., NEJM 2013/2018

Current plan (MODERATE evidence):
- Meta-analysis 14 cohorts (n=525,047): OR 0.87 (0.70-1.07), trend not significant
- German Nutrition Society 2025: protein intake NOT associated with CHD/stroke
- Yogurt consumption: RR 0.89 (0.81-0.98) for CVD mortality

Verdict: Mediterranean has stronger CVD evidence. Shared elements (EVOO, fish,
legumes, vegetables) likely confer similar benefit.

================================================================
2. ALL-CAUSE MORTALITY
================================================================

Mediterranean (MODERATE evidence):
- 29 cohorts (n=1,676,901): HR 0.90 (0.89-0.91) per 2-point adherence
  Source: Sofi et al., 2019
- Exclusively observational. No RCT shows significant mortality reduction.

Current plan (MODERATE-STRONG evidence):
- BMJ 2020 (n=715,128, 113,039 deaths): total protein HR 0.94 (0.89-0.99)
  Plant protein HR 0.92 (0.87-0.97)
- Each 3% energy substituted animal->plant protein: HR 0.88 (0.85-0.91)
- Plant-to-animal ratio >0.50 showed greatest benefit (AJCN 2024)

Current plan ratio: ~30-40% plant protein. Evidence supports pushing toward 50%+.

Verdict: Similar evidence quality. Increasing plant protein ratio would improve
mortality outcomes.

================================================================
3. INFLAMMATION (CRP, IL-6)
================================================================

Mediterranean (MODERATE-HIGH evidence):
- 33 RCTs (n=3,476): significant reductions in hs-CRP and IL-6
  IL-6 mean difference -1.07 pg/mL
  Source: Nutrition Reviews 2025
- EVOO-supplemented MedDiet reduced both markers
- No significant effect on TNF-alpha

Current plan: anti-inflammatory framework (fatty fish 2-3x/week, EVOO, turmeric,
ginger, colorful vegetables, no seed oils/fried/processed) maps directly to the
interventions tested in these RCTs.

Verdict: Equivalent. Same mechanisms implemented.

================================================================
4. TYPE 2 DIABETES PREVENTION
================================================================

Mediterranean (MODERATE evidence):
- 25 studies (n=991,878): HR 0.92 (0.90-0.94) per 2-point adherence
- In existing T2D: HbA1c reduced by 0.307%

Current plan (MODERATE-STRONG evidence):
- Yogurt and T2D: RR 0.73 (0.70-0.76) -- 27% risk reduction
- Yogurt and metabolic syndrome: RR 0.80 (0.74-0.87) -- 20% reduction
- High protein improves glycemic control via satiety and reduced postprandial spikes

Verdict: Current plan may have advantage due to heavy yogurt intake carrying
strong, replicated T2D risk reduction exceeding general Mediterranean association.

================================================================
5. COGNITIVE DECLINE / DEMENTIA
================================================================

Mediterranean (MODERATE evidence):
- 23 studies: cognitive impairment HR 0.82 (0.75-0.89)
  Alzheimer's HR 0.70 (0.60-0.82)
  Source: GeroScience 2025

Current plan: no direct evidence for high-protein diets and cognition.
Shared protective components (fish, EVOO, vegetables, nuts) are present.
Absence of whole grains may matter -- B vitamins and folate support cognition.
Legume intake partially compensates.

Verdict: Mediterranean has direct evidence. Current plan shares key components
but lacks whole-grain contribution.

================================================================
6. CANCER RISK
================================================================

Mediterranean (LOW-MODERATE evidence):
- 117 studies (n=3.2 million): cancer mortality RR 0.87 (0.82-0.92)
  Colorectal RR 0.83 (0.76-0.90). All observational.
  Source: Morze et al., European Journal of Nutrition 2020

Current plan risk factors:
- Red meat 2x/week: JAMA Internal Medicine (n=29,682, 19 years)
  Per 2 servings/week unprocessed: all-cause HR 1.03 (1.01-1.05)
  Absolute risk increase <1% over 30 years
  At ~20g/day average, well below colorectal cancer threshold (~50g/day)
- Eggs 1-2/day: dose-response meta-analysis (n=2.7 million)
  All-cause mortality risk elevated above ~1.5 eggs/day
  Cancer mortality RR 1.13 (1.06-1.20) per egg/day
  Geographic confounding significant (dietary context matters)

Verdict: Mediterranean shows modest cancer protection. Current plan adds small
absolute risk from red meat/eggs. Limit eggs to 1/day.

================================================================
7. WEIGHT MANAGEMENT AND MUSCLE PRESERVATION
================================================================

Mediterranean (MODERATE evidence):
- 16 RCTs (n=3,436): weight loss -1.75 kg vs controls
  With exercise: -4.01 kg
  Not designed as weight-loss diet. Typical protein ~60-80g/day.

Current plan (STRONG evidence):
- 49 studies: significantly decreased hunger, desire to eat, prospective consumption
- Fat-free mass preservation during weight loss: consistently demonstrated
- Morton et al.: muscle protein synthesis threshold at 1.62 g/kg/day
  Current plan at 1.4-1.6 g/kg approaches optimal ceiling
- CRITICAL: BMC Geriatrics 2025 -- protein WITHOUT exercise had NO significant
  effect on lean body mass in older adults. Must combine with resistance training.

Verdict: Current plan substantially superior for weight management and muscle
preservation. Main advantage over standard Mediterranean.

================================================================
8. KIDNEY FUNCTION
================================================================

Current plan protein level (STRONG evidence -- no harm in healthy adults):
- 28 RCTs (n=1,358): no adverse GFR effect. Source: Devries et al., 2018
- 6 studies (n=148,051): higher protein = 18% LOWER CKD risk (RR 0.82, 0.71-0.94)
- CAVEAT: in impaired kidney function, >1.4 g/kg/day = faster eGFR decline
  Monitor with regular bloodwork

================================================================
9. GUT MICROBIOME
================================================================

Mediterranean (MODERATE evidence):
- NU-AGE RCT (n=612, 1 year): increased beneficial taxa (F. prausnitzii,
  Akkermansia, Bifidobacterium), increased SCFA production
  Source: Ghosh et al., Gut 2020

Current plan: likely similar effects from shared elements. Daily legumes and
yogurt are independently favorable for microbiome diversity.

================================================================
10. WHOLE GRAINS -- BIGGEST GAP IN CURRENT PLAN
================================================================

Evidence (HIGH certainty per GRADE):
- Meta-analysis 24 articles (n=1.6 million):
  Per 30g/day whole grains: CVD RR 0.92 (0.88-0.96)
  All-cause mortality RR 0.94 (0.92-0.97)
- RCTs confirm whole grains improve total cholesterol, LDL, HbA1c, CRP

Current plan eliminates grains entirely. High legume intake compensates for
fiber (29-33g/day) but specific cereal fiber CVD associations may not be
fully replicated by legume fiber. No RCT compares grain-free + high-legume
vs. grain-inclusive diets.

================================================================
EVIDENCE-BASED RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================

1. ADD WHOLE GRAINS
   Evidence for mortality and CVD benefit is high-certainty. Even 1-2
   servings/day of oats, barley, or bulgur captures most benefit.
   Oats are on approved starch list but absent from weekly menus.

2. INCREASE PLANT-TO-ANIMAL PROTEIN RATIO TOWARD 50%+
   Substituting 3% calories animal->plant = 12% lower all-cause mortality.
   Add tofu/tempeh/edamame to more meals.

3. LIMIT EGGS TO 1/DAY
   Threshold data: risk elevation above 1.5 eggs/day. On 2-egg breakfast
   days, avoid eggs at other meals.

4. EXPAND FRUIT VARIETY
   Add citrus (flavanones) beyond blueberries and apples. Evidence is
   low quality but cost is zero.

5. COTTAGE CHEESE IS NOT YOGURT
   Not fermented the same way, lacks live cultures. Does not carry same
   metabolic benefits as Greek yogurt. Favor yogurt when choosing.
