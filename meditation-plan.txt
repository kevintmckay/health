DAILY MEDITATION PLAN
=======================

Yes, meditation will help. It is one of the most studied interventions
for anxiety, and directly supports the CBT work. The research is clear:

  - Reduces baseline anxiety levels (not just in the moment)
  - Improves ability to notice urges without acting on them
  - Lowers cortisol (stress hormone that drives belly fat storage)
  - Reduces pain perception (relevant to your sciatica)
  - Builds the pause between feeling and reaction — the exact skill
    you need for emotional eating

You do not need to clear your mind. You do not need to be good at it.
You just need to sit there and practice noticing.


THE ONLY TECHNIQUE YOU NEED TO START
======================================

Breathing awareness. That is it. Everything else is optional.

  1. Sit in a chair or lie on your back (whatever your back tolerates)
  2. Close your eyes or look at a spot on the floor
  3. Breathe normally — do not force it
  4. Pay attention to the feeling of air coming in and going out
  5. When your mind wanders (it will, constantly), notice that it
     wandered and bring attention back to breathing
  6. That moment of noticing and returning IS the exercise
     It is not a failure. It is a rep.


6-WEEK PROGRESSION
====================

WEEK 1: 3 MINUTES
  - Once per day
  - Set a timer on your phone
  - Sit quietly and focus on breathing
  - That is the entire practice
  - If 3 minutes feels hard, that is normal. Do it anyway.

WEEK 2: 5 MINUTES
  - Once per day
  - Same thing. Sit. Breathe. Notice when your mind wanders. Return.
  - You will spend most of the time distracted. That is fine.

WEEK 3: 5 MINUTES + BODY SCAN
  - Start with 2 minutes of breathing
  - Then slowly scan your attention from your feet to your head
  - At each area, just notice what you feel (tension, warmth, nothing)
  - Do not try to fix or change anything. Just notice.
  - This builds the skill of observing discomfort without reacting —
    the same skill that helps with urge surfing.

WEEK 4: 7 MINUTES
  - 3 min breathing + 4 min body scan
  - You may start noticing anxiety as a physical sensation (tight chest,
    clenched jaw, shallow breathing). This is progress. You are getting
    better at catching it early.

WEEK 5: 10 MINUTES
  - 3 min breathing + 5 min body scan + 2 min open awareness
  - Open awareness: stop focusing on anything specific. Just sit and
    let sounds, thoughts, and sensations come and go. Watch them like
    cars passing on a road. You are the observer, not the traffic.

WEEK 6+: 10-15 MINUTES
  - Find the mix that works for you
  - Some days all breathing. Some days body scan. Some days just sitting.
  - The habit matters more than the technique.


WHEN TO MEDITATE
==================

Best times (pick one and make it consistent):
  - Morning, right after waking (before phone, before coffee)
  - After your walk
  - During your pre-bed wind-down routine

If you can only do one: morning. It sets your baseline anxiety lower
for the entire day, which means fewer eating triggers.


THE ANXIETY EMERGENCY TOOL: 4-7-8 BREATHING
=============================================

This is not meditation. This is a rapid calm-down technique for when
anxiety spikes and you feel an urge to eat right now.

  1. Breathe in through your nose for 4 seconds
  2. Hold for 7 seconds
  3. Breathe out slowly through your mouth for 8 seconds
  4. Repeat 3-4 times

This activates your parasympathetic nervous system (the opposite of
fight-or-flight). It physically slows your heart rate and lowers
adrenaline. Use it before you open the fridge.


THE EATING-SPECIFIC MEDITATION: RAIN
======================================

Use this when you feel an emotional eating urge. It pairs directly
with the CBT urge surfing technique.

  R - Recognize: "I'm having an urge to eat."
  A - Allow: "This urge is here. I don't have to fight it or feed it."
  I - Investigate: "Where do I feel this in my body? What emotion is
      underneath? What am I actually needing right now?"
  N - Non-identification: "This urge is not me. It is something passing
      through me. I can watch it without becoming it."

Takes 2-3 minutes. Can be done anywhere — sitting at your desk,
standing in the kitchen, in your car.


COMMON OBSTACLES
==================

"I can't stop my thoughts."
  You are not supposed to. The practice is noticing them and returning
  to your breath. A meditation full of distractions where you kept
  returning is a successful meditation.

"I feel more anxious when I sit still."
  Normal in the first 1-2 weeks. You are not creating anxiety — you are
  noticing anxiety that was always there but covered up by distractions.
  It gets better. If it feels overwhelming, open your eyes and shorten
  the session.

"I keep forgetting to do it."
  Anchor it to something you already do. Right after brushing teeth.
  Right after your morning coffee is poured. Right after your walk.
  Same time, same place, every day.

"I fell asleep."
  Sit upright instead of lying down. Also a sign you need more sleep
  (see sleep plan).

"I missed a day / a week / a month."
  Sit down for 3 minutes today. You are back.


APPS THAT HELP (ALL HAVE FREE OPTIONS)
========================================
  - Insight Timer (free, large library of guided meditations)
  - UCLA Mindful (free, research-based, simple)
  - Headspace (paid, very structured for beginners)
  - Calm (paid, good sleep content too)

You do not need an app. Timer on your phone works fine. But guided
sessions can help in the first few weeks if sitting in silence feels
too hard.


HOW THIS CONNECTS TO EVERYTHING ELSE
=======================================
  Meditation + CBT guide  = handles the anxiety and emotional eating
  Walking                 = burns stress hormones and improves mood
  Sleep plan              = reduces baseline anxiety and pain sensitivity
  Diet plan               = gives you a simple structure so food decisions
                            are not emotional decisions
  Core exercises          = reduces pain, which reduces stress, which
                            reduces emotional eating

They all reinforce each other. Start with what you can. Add over time.
