EINKORN SOURDOUGH BREAD — WHY IT FITS THE PLAN
=================================================

Fresh-ground einkorn sourdough is probably the single healthiest bread
a person can eat. It fits well as your starch portion at meals.


WHAT IS EINKORN
=================
Einkorn (Triticum monococcum) is one of the earliest cultivated wheat
varieties. It has never been hybridized like modern wheat. Its nutritional
profile and gluten structure are significantly different from what you
find in store-bought bread.


EINKORN VS MODERN WHEAT
=========================

Higher protein:
  - Einkorn: 18-20% protein vs modern wheat: 10-14%
  - Aligns with the high-protein diet approach

More nutrients:
  - Higher in carotenoids (especially lutein)
  - Higher in B vitamins, zinc, and iron
  - Higher antioxidant content (relevant for anti-inflammatory goals)

Lower glycemic response:
  - Studies show einkorn raises blood sugar less sharply than modern wheat
  - Less spike-crash-hunger cycle

Different gluten:
  - Einkorn gluten is weaker and more fragile than modern wheat gluten
  - Easier to digest for many people
  - NOT safe for celiacs — still contains gluten


FRESHLY GRINDING THE BERRIES
==============================
Grinding your own einkorn berries right before baking preserves the germ
and bran oils that oxidize and go rancid in pre-ground flour. Store-bought
whole wheat flour has been sitting around losing nutrients. Fresh-ground
retains the full nutritional profile.


WHY SOURDOUGH MAKES IT BETTER
================================

Sourdough fermentation meaningfully improves einkorn bread in ways
directly relevant to this health plan:

Lowers glycemic index further:
  - Organic acids produced during fermentation slow starch digestion
  - Less blood sugar spike means less crash, less hunger, less afternoon
    snacking urge

Breaks down phytic acid:
  - Phytic acid in grains blocks absorption of minerals (iron, zinc,
    magnesium, calcium)
  - Long sourdough fermentation reduces phytic acid by 50-70%
  - You absorb significantly more of the nutrients already higher in einkorn

Pre-digests the gluten:
  - Lactobacilli in sourdough culture partially break down gluten proteins
  - Combined with einkorn's already weaker gluten, this makes it
    substantially easier on the gut

Produces beneficial organic acids:
  - Lactic and acetic acid feed healthy gut bacteria
  - Better gut health is linked to lower systemic inflammation
  - Relevant for sciatica and hip pain

Longer satiety:
  - The combination of lower glycemic response and acid/fiber matrix
    keeps you full longer per calorie than regular bread


PRACTICAL NOTES
=================
- Longer fermentation = more benefits. A 12-24 hour bulk ferment gets
  the most phytic acid reduction and gluten breakdown.
- Einkorn is stickier and weaker than modern wheat dough. It will not
  rise as high. That is normal. Do not add modern wheat to compensate.
- It keeps well and actually improves in digestibility a day after baking.
- Store in a bread box or paper bag, not plastic (keeps the crust intact).


HOW IT FITS THE DIET PLAN
============================
- It still counts as your fist-sized starch portion at meals
- Healthier bread is still bread — calorie-dense (~80-100 cal/slice)
  and easy to overeat, especially fresh out of the oven
- Keep it to 1-2 slices per meal
- Best used at breakfast (eggs + einkorn toast) or lunch
- The nutritional advantage over store-bought bread is real but the
  portion rule still applies
