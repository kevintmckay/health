CBT GUIDE FOR ANXIETY-DRIVEN EATING
======================================

Customize this file with your own notes and insights.
Place your personal version in the personal/ folder.

THE ANXIETY-EATING CYCLE
==========================
Trigger > Anxious thought > Uncomfortable feeling > Urge to eat >
Eat (temporary relief) > Guilt > Repeat

The goal: break the chain between feeling and eating.

STEP 1: AWARENESS (Week 1-2)
-------------------------------
Log any eating outside planned meals:
- Time, what you ate, hunger type (physical vs emotional)
- Feeling before, trigger, feeling after
- No judgment — just collecting data

STEP 2: CATCH THE THOUGHT (Week 3-4)
---------------------------------------
Common automatic thoughts:
- "I need something to take the edge off"
- "I've had a hard day, I deserve this"
- "One won't hurt" / "I'll start fresh tomorrow"
When you notice an urge, ask: "What thought just happened?"

STEP 3: CHALLENGE & REFRAME (Week 5-6)
-----------------------------------------
Run the thought through these questions:
1. Is this actually true, or does it just feel true?
2. What would I tell a friend who said this?
3. Will eating actually solve what I'm anxious about?
4. How will I feel 30 minutes after eating this?

STEP 4: URGE SURFING (Week 7-8)
----------------------------------
1. Notice the urge — "I'm having an urge. That's fine."
2. Rate it 1-10
3. Set a 15-minute timer
4. Do something else: walk, water, breathe, call someone
5. Re-rate after timer — it will almost always be lower

THINKING TRAPS
================
- All-or-nothing: "I ate a cookie so the day is ruined"
- Catastrophizing: "I'll never be able to change"
- Emotional reasoning: "I feel like a failure, so I must be one"
- Permission giving: "I've been good all week"

Reality: Feelings are not facts. Progress, not perfection.
