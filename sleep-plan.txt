SLEEP PLAN — FIXING YOUR FOUNDATION
======================================

Goal: 7-8 hours of quality sleep on a consistent schedule
Why: Sleep directly affects pain sensitivity, weight loss, recovery, and motivation


PICK YOUR TIMES
=================
Choose a wake time and work backward 8 hours. Stick to these 7 days a week.

Example schedules:
  Early bird:    10:00 PM bedtime  ->  6:00 AM wake
  Standard:      10:30 PM bedtime  ->  6:30 AM wake
  Night owl:     11:00 PM bedtime  ->  7:00 AM wake

Your schedule:
  Bedtime:  ___________
  Wake up:  ___________

The single most important rule: Keep your wake time the same every day,
including weekends. This anchors your circadian rhythm faster than anything else.


WEEK 1-2: SET THE ANCHOR
==========================
- Pick your wake time and set an alarm. Get up at that time no matter what.
- Get outside within 30 minutes of waking. 10 minutes of daylight resets
  your internal clock. Overcast days still work.
- Stop caffeine after 12:00 PM (noon). Caffeine has a 6-8 hour half-life.
  That 3 PM coffee is still in your system at 11 PM.
- No naps longer than 20 minutes. If you must nap, before 2 PM only.


WEEK 3-4: BUILD THE WIND-DOWN
===============================
Start a simple pre-bed routine 30-60 minutes before your bedtime.

30-60 min before bed:
  - Dim the lights in your house (overhead lights off, lamps on)
  - Screens off or use night mode (phone, TV, tablet)
  - No work email, news, or stressful content

15-30 min before bed:
  - Light stretching or the McGill Big 3 at low intensity (optional)
  - Read a book (paper, not screen)
  - Warm shower or bath (the cooling effect afterward promotes sleep)

At bedtime:
  - Lights out
  - Phone face down or in another room
  - If not asleep in 20 minutes, get up and sit quietly in dim light
    until you feel drowsy, then return to bed


YOUR BEDROOM
==============
Make your room work for sleep, not against it.

Temperature:
  - 65-68 degrees F is ideal for most people
  - Cool room + warm blanket is the best combination
  - A fan helps with both temperature and white noise

Darkness:
  - As dark as possible
  - Blackout curtains if street light comes in
  - Cover any LED lights (alarm clock, charger, etc.)
  - If blackout curtains are not an option, use a sleep mask

Noise:
  - Quiet or consistent background noise (fan, white noise machine)
  - Earplugs if you have a noisy environment
  - Phone on silent (not vibrate)

Bed:
  - Use your bed for sleep only (not TV, phone scrolling, work)
  - Read in a chair, not in bed — move to bed only when drowsy
  - This trains your brain that bed = sleep


SCIATICA AND SLEEP POSITIONS
==============================
Back pain and sciatica can make sleep difficult. These positions reduce
spinal stress:

On your back:
  - Place a pillow under your knees
  - This flattens the lumbar curve and takes pressure off the spine
  - Best position for most people with sciatica

On your side:
  - Place a pillow between your knees
  - Keep knees slightly bent
  - This keeps your hips aligned and prevents rotation

Avoid:
  - Stomach sleeping (forces spinal extension and neck rotation)
  - Twisted or half-stomach positions


THINGS THAT WRECK SLEEP
=========================
- Alcohol: Feels like it helps but fragments sleep and kills deep sleep stages.
  If you drink, stop at least 3 hours before bed.
- Large meals within 2 hours of bedtime (acid reflux, discomfort)
- Exercise within 2 hours of bedtime (raises core temperature)
- Checking the clock when you wake at night (creates anxiety)
  Turn the clock away from you.
- Blue light from screens after your wind-down starts
- Irregular schedule (sleeping in on weekends undoes the whole week)


TROUBLESHOOTING
================

Can't fall asleep:
  - Get up after 20 minutes. Sit in dim light. Return when drowsy.
  - Do not lie there frustrated. That trains your brain that bed = awake.
  - Try a body scan: mentally relax from toes to head, one body part at a time.

Wake up in the middle of the night:
  - Normal. Most people wake briefly 2-3 times per night.
  - Do not check your phone or the clock.
  - If still awake after 20 minutes, get up briefly then return.

Wake up too early:
  - If within 30 min of your alarm, just get up.
  - If hours early, check if you are going to bed too early.

Still exhausted after 8 hours:
  - Could be sleep apnea. Very common at this weight and age.
  - Symptoms: loud snoring, gasping at night, dry mouth on waking,
    headaches in the morning.
  - Talk to your doctor. A sleep study can diagnose it.
  - This is worth checking — untreated sleep apnea sabotages everything else.


PROGRESS MARKERS
==================
Week 1:    Consistent wake time established
Week 2:    Caffeine cutoff and morning light exposure are habits
Week 3-4:  Wind-down routine in place, falling asleep faster
Week 6+:   Waking before your alarm naturally
Week 8+:   Noticeably more energy, less pain sensitivity, better workouts
