WALKING PLAN — 12 WEEK PROGRESSION
=====================================

Starting point: Deconditioned, gets winded fast, sciatica risk
Goal: Build to 30-45 minutes of continuous walking most days


BEFORE YOU START
=================
- Wear supportive shoes with good cushioning (replace if worn out)
- Walk on flat, even surfaces to start (sidewalks, tracks, malls)
- Avoid hills and uneven terrain until week 6+
- Walk at a pace where you can hold a conversation but feel slightly winded
- Stop immediately if you get sharp pain or radiating leg symptoms

AFTER 12 WEEKS
================
- Maintain 30-45 min walks 5-6 days per week
- Start adding gentle hills or inclines if sciatica allows
- Increase pace gradually rather than adding more time
- Consider adding a longer walk on weekends (45-60 min)


SCIATICA MANAGEMENT DURING WALKS
==================================
- If symptoms start during a walk, stop and do a standing back extension
  (hands on hips, gently lean back, hold 5 seconds, repeat 3-5 times)
- Shorten the next walk by 5 minutes and rebuild from there
- Avoid walking through radiating leg pain — mild back tightness is ok,
  nerve pain is not
- If a flare-up happens, take 1-2 rest days then resume at a shorter duration
- Do not restart the entire plan — just drop back a week and continue


TIPS
=====
- Same time every day builds the habit fastest (morning is best for most people)
- Bad weather: walk in a mall, big box store, or on a treadmill
- Bring your phone for music or podcasts — makes time pass faster
- Track your walks (phone app, notebook, calendar check marks)
- A missed day is not a failure. Just walk the next day.
- Soreness is normal. Sharp pain is not. Know the difference.
