SUPPLEMENT GUIDE
================

Profile: 57 years old, 240 lbs, weight loss, sciatica/chronic pain, high protein diet
Critical first step: GET BLOODWORK before long-term supplementation


TIER 1: TAKE THESE (HIGH PRIORITY, STRONG EVIDENCE)
====================================================

1. VITAMIN D3 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
------------------

WHY YOU SPECIFICALLY NEED THIS:
- Age 57: Vitamin D production decreases with age
- Limited mobility/outdoor time: Chronic pain = less sun exposure
- Chronic pain: Low vitamin D worsens pain sensitivity and inflammation
- Weight loss: Vitamin D is fat-soluble, stored in fat tissue (harder to access when overweight)
- Research: Low vitamin D strongly associated with chronic musculoskeletal pain

THIS WAS THE #1 GAP IDENTIFIED IN YOUR DIET PLAN

DOSAGE:
- 2000-4000 IU per day (50-100 mcg)
- Start with 2000 IU if you've never taken it
- Increase to 4000 IU if bloodwork shows deficiency

TIMING:
- Take with a meal containing fat (breakfast with eggs, or with fish oil)
- Fat-soluble vitamin = needs fat for absorption

FORM:
- Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol), NOT D2
- Softgel or capsule

COST: $10-15 for 3-6 month supply

SAFETY: Very safe at these doses, toxicity only at 10,000+ IU daily long-term

BLOODWORK: Get vitamin D level checked in 8-12 weeks
- Target: 40-60 ng/mL
- Deficient: <20 ng/mL
- Insufficient: 20-30 ng/mL
- Optimal: 40-60 ng/mL

BRANDS: NatureWise, NOW Foods, Thorne


2. OMEGA-3 FISH OIL (EPA/DHA) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
------------------------------------

WHY YOU NEED THIS:
- Anti-inflammatory: Directly reduces inflammatory markers (critical for sciatica/hip pain)
- Research: 2-3g EPA+DHA per day reduces pain in chronic conditions
- Backup for weeks you don't eat fatty fish

WHEN TO TAKE:
- IF eating salmon/sardines 2-3x per week: SKIP supplement (you get enough from food)
- IF eating fish 0-1x per week: TAKE supplement

DOSAGE:
- 2-3g combined EPA+DHA per day
- Example label: "EPA 1200mg + DHA 800mg" = 2000mg total ✓
- NOT total fish oil (3000mg fish oil might only have 900mg EPA+DHA - READ THE LABEL)

TIMING:
- With food (better absorption, less fishy burps)
- Take with dinner or largest meal

FORM:
- Triglyceride form (best absorption) > ethyl ester form
- Molecularly distilled (removes mercury/PCBs)
- Third-party tested (IFOS, USP, ConsumerLab)

COST: $20-40/month for quality brand

BRANDS: Nordic Naturals, Carlson, Viva Naturals, WHC UnoCardio
- Look for third-party testing certification on label

STORAGE: REFRIGERATE after opening (prevents oxidation/rancidity)

CAUTION: Check with doctor if on blood thinners (fish oil has mild blood-thinning effect)

RECOMMENDATION:
- Eat salmon 2-3x/week (already planned) = skip supplement most weeks ✓
- Keep fish oil on hand for weeks you skip fish
- Take 2-3g on non-fish days


3. MAGNESIUM ⭐⭐⭐⭐
-----------------

WHY YOU NEED THIS:
- Sleep quality: Improves deep sleep (your sleep plan is critical)
- Muscle recovery: Supports recovery from walking/exercise
- Stress/anxiety: Calming effect (helps with emotional eating triggers)
- Constipation prevention: High protein diet needs this
- Common deficiency: 50%+ of adults don't get enough from diet

DOSAGE:
- 300-400mg elemental magnesium per day
- Men 50+: RDA is 420mg

FORMS (THIS MATTERS):
- Magnesium glycinate: BEST for sleep, muscle recovery, least GI issues (USE THIS)
- Magnesium citrate: Good for constipation (can cause loose stools)
- Magnesium oxide: Cheap but poorly absorbed (AVOID)
- Magnesium threonate: Best for brain/anxiety but expensive

TIMING:
- BEFORE BED (helps sleep, relaxation)
- 1-2 hours before bedtime with your cottage cheese

START LOW:
- Week 1-2: 200mg before bed
- Week 3+: Increase to 400mg if no loose stools

COST: $15-20 for 2-3 month supply

SIDE EFFECTS:
- Loose stools if dose too high
- If this happens, reduce dose or split into 200mg twice daily

FOOD SOURCES (you get some from diet):
- Spinach, pumpkin seeds, almonds, black beans
- Hard to get 400mg from food alone

BRANDS: Doctor's Best, KAL, Pure Encapsulations


TIER 2: CONSIDER THESE (DEPENDS ON DIET/BLOODWORK)
===================================================

4. FIBER SUPPLEMENT (PSYLLIUM HUSK) ⭐⭐⭐
--------------------------------------

WHY YOU MIGHT NEED THIS:
- High protein diet (120-140g) without enough fiber = constipation
- Target: 25-30g fiber/day
- IF eating beans daily + vegetables: You're probably fine, skip supplement ✓
- IF constipated: Add supplement

DOSAGE:
- 5-10g psyllium husk per day
- Start with 5g, increase gradually

TIMING:
- With breakfast or before bed
- TAKE 2+ HOURS AWAY from other supplements/medications (fiber blocks absorption)
- Drink LOTS of water with it (16 oz minimum)

FORM: Psyllium husk powder (Metamucil unflavored, or generic)

COST: $10-15 for 1-2 month supply

RECOMMENDATION:
- Try diet first (beans daily, vegetables, adequate water)
- Only add if still constipated after 2 weeks
- You're eating lentils/beans daily, you probably won't need this ✓


5. PROTEIN POWDER (PEA PROTEIN) ⭐⭐
--------------------------------

WHY YOU MIGHT NEED THIS:
- Backup for days you fall short on protein from food
- Emergency meal (shake + banana = 2 min breakfast)

WHEN TO USE:
- Days you can't meal prep
- Didn't eat enough protein at meals
- Post-resistance training (convenient)

DOSAGE:
- 0-2 scoops per day MAXIMUM
- 1 scoop = 20-25g protein
- PRIORITIZE WHOLE FOOD PROTEIN FIRST

YOU HAVE PEA PROTEIN ✓
- Use as backup only, not daily staple
- Whole foods (eggs, chicken, yogurt) are better


6. CREATINE MONOHYDRATE ⭐⭐⭐
--------------------------

WHY YOU MIGHT BENEFIT:
- Age 57: Preserves muscle mass and strength during aging
- Exercise performance: Helps with resistance training progression
- Research: Safe, effective for older adults
- Cognitive benefits: May improve brain function (emerging research)

DOSAGE:
- 5g per day (1 teaspoon)
- Every day, doesn't matter when
- No loading phase needed

TIMING: Anytime, with or without food

COST: $15-20 for 6+ month supply (very cheap)

FORM: Creatine monohydrate (cheapest, most studied)

SIDE EFFECTS:
- Water retention (2-4 lbs - this is normal, not fat)
- Drink more water (already doing this on high protein)

RECOMMENDATION:
- WAIT UNTIL WEEK 4-6 (after adding resistance exercises)
- Helps you progress faster in strength
- Very safe, well-studied
- Optional but useful

BRANDS: Optimum Nutrition, Bulk Supplements


7. VITAMIN B12 ⭐⭐
---------------

WHY YOU MIGHT NEED THIS:
- Age 50+: Absorption decreases with age
- Fatigue/energy: B12 deficiency common in older adults
- IF eating meat/eggs/dairy daily: Probably fine, get bloodwork to check

DOSAGE:
- 500-1000 mcg per day (if deficient)
- OR 2500 mcg twice per week

TIMING: Morning with breakfast

FORM: Methylcobalamin or cyanocobalamin (both fine)

COST: $10 for 6+ month supply

RECOMMENDATION:
- GET BLOODWORK FIRST - don't guess
- You eat eggs, chicken, yogurt daily - likely NOT deficient
- Only supplement if blood test shows low B12


TIER 3: MAYBE HELPFUL (WEAK/MIXED EVIDENCE)
============================================

8. GLUCOSAMINE/CHONDROITIN ⭐⭐
---------------------------

WHY PEOPLE TAKE IT: Joint pain, arthritis

EVIDENCE: Mixed - some studies show benefit for knee osteoarthritis, others show no effect

RECOMMENDATION:
- Try diet changes first (anti-inflammatory foods, omega-3, weight loss)
- If still have joint pain after 12 weeks, consider trying
- 3-month trial to see if it helps

DOSAGE: 1500mg glucosamine + 1200mg chondroitin per day

COST: $20-30/month

NOT A PRIORITY FOR YOU RIGHT NOW


9. TURMERIC/CURCUMIN ⭐⭐⭐
------------------------

WHY PEOPLE TAKE IT: Anti-inflammatory for joint pain

EVIDENCE: Moderate - some studies show benefit for osteoarthritis/inflammation

PROBLEM: Very poorly absorbed unless combined with black pepper (piperine) or special formulation

RECOMMENDATION:
- ADD TURMERIC TO COOKING (cheaper, easier) - your diet plan mentions this ✓
- Supplement only if cooking doesn't help pain
- Needs to be high-quality with piperine

DOSAGE: 500-1000mg curcumin with piperine, 2x per day

COST: $25-35/month for quality brand

BEST APPROACH:
- Add turmeric powder to lentil sauté, scrambled eggs, roasted vegetables
- Try food form first for 8-12 weeks
- Supplement only if still having significant pain


TIER 4: DON'T WASTE YOUR MONEY
===============================

MULTIVITAMINS ❌
- "Shotgun approach" - gives you things you don't need
- Doses too low to matter for things you do need
- Better to supplement specific deficiencies (vitamin D, etc.)
- Exception: If you absolutely won't take individual vitamins, basic multivitamin better than nothing

TESTOSTERONE BOOSTERS ❌
- No evidence they work
- Expensive
- If you have low testosterone, get actual prescription (doctor can check with bloodwork)

FAT BURNERS/THERMOGENICS ❌
- Don't work
- Often have stimulants (mess with sleep, anxiety)
- Waste of money
- Calorie deficit + protein + walking = all you need

COLLAGEN SUPPLEMENTS ❌
- Weak evidence for joint pain
- Expensive
- Just eat protein (your body makes collagen from amino acids)
- Not worth the cost

PROBIOTICS ❌ (MOSTLY)
- Fermented foods are better (sauerkraut, yogurt)
- Specific strains matter - most supplements have wrong strains
- Expensive
- Exception: If you have IBS or took antibiotics recently, then consider

BCAAs (BRANCHED CHAIN AMINO ACIDS) ❌
- You're eating 120-140g protein/day - you don't need these
- BCAAs are already in your food (eggs, chicken, yogurt)
- Waste of money

PRE-WORKOUT SUPPLEMENTS ❌
- You're doing walking + bodyweight exercises, not heavy lifting
- Just caffeine (drink coffee if you want)
- Expensive
- Can mess with sleep/anxiety

QUALITY MATTERS (HOW TO BUY)
=============================

LOOK FOR:
- Third-party tested: USP, NSF, ConsumerLab, IFOS (for fish oil)
- Avoid proprietary blends (hidden doses)
- Check expiration dates
- Store properly (fish oil in fridge, others in cool, dry place)

GOOD BRANDS:
- Vitamin D: NatureWise, NOW Foods, Thorne
- Magnesium: Doctor's Best, KAL, Pure Encapsulations
- Fish oil: Nordic Naturals, Carlson, Viva Naturals
- Creatine: Optimum Nutrition, Bulk Supplements

WHERE TO BUY:
- Amazon (check reviews, third-party testing)
- Costco (good prices on basics)
- iHerb (wide selection)
- Local health food store

AVOID: Gas stations, dollar stores, brands with outrageous claims


WHAT NOT TO DO
==============

❌ Don't take supplements on empty stomach (can cause nausea)
❌ Don't take fiber with other supplements (blocks absorption)
❌ Don't take more than recommended dose (more ≠ better)
❌ Don't expect instant results (vitamins take weeks to build up)
❌ Don't skip bloodwork (you need to know your levels)
❌ Don't buy based on marketing claims (look for third-party testing)
