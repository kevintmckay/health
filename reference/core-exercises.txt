CORE, HIP, AND RESISTANCE EXERCISES
======================================

Three categories of exercises, all spine-safe:
  1. McGill Big 3 (core stability)
  2. Hip exercises (addresses hip pain and sciatica contributors)
  3. Basic resistance work (preserves muscle during weight loss)


=====================================================================
PART 1: MCGILL BIG 3 — CORE STABILITY
=====================================================================

These three exercises come from Dr. Stuart McGill's spine biomechanics
research at the University of Waterloo. They are designed to build core
endurance while keeping the spine in a safe, neutral position. No spinal
flexion under load. No sit-ups. No crunches.


1. CURL-UP
===========

Starting position:
- Lie on your back
- One knee bent with foot flat on the floor
- Other leg straight
- Place both hands under the small of your lower back (this locks your spine neutral)

The movement:
- Lift only your head and shoulders slightly off the floor
- Do NOT tuck your chin or curl your spine
- Think about lifting your chest toward the ceiling about 1 inch
- Your lower back should not move at all — your hands underneath prevent this
- Hold for 6-8 seconds
- Lower back down

Key points:
- This is a very small movement. If it looks like a crunch you are doing it wrong.
- Your elbows can rest on the floor or hover slightly
- Switch which leg is bent halfway through your sets
- Breathe normally during the hold

Sets/reps to start: 3 sets of 6 reps (6-8 second holds each)


2. SIDE PLANK
==============

Starting position (modified):
- Lie on your side
- Prop up on your elbow directly under your shoulder
- Knees bent at 90 degrees (this is the modified version)
- Top arm resting along your side or on your hip

The movement:
- Lift your hips off the ground so your body forms a straight line
  from your knees to your shoulders
- Hold this position for 6-8 seconds
- Lower back down
- Complete all reps on one side then switch

Progression:
- Modified (from knees) — start here
- Full side plank (from feet) — progress to this when modified feels easy
- You can also increase hold times before adding reps

Key points:
- Do not let your hips sag or rotate forward/backward
- Keep your body in a straight line
- Breathe normally
- If this bothers your shoulder, check that your elbow is directly under it

Sets/reps to start: 3 sets of 6 reps each side (6-8 second holds each)


3. BIRD-DOG
=============

Starting position:
- On your hands and knees (all fours)
- Hands directly under shoulders
- Knees directly under hips
- Back flat — neutral spine (imagine a glass of water sitting on your lower back)

The movement:
- Slowly extend your right arm straight forward and your left leg straight back
  at the same time
- Arm and leg should be in line with your body, not raised above it
- Hold for 6-8 seconds
- Return to start
- Repeat with left arm and right leg

Key points:
- The goal is ZERO movement in your torso. Nothing shifts or rotates.
- If your hips rock side to side, reduce range of motion (don't extend as far)
- Make a fist with the extended hand (McGill's recommendation for better activation)
- Sweep the floor with your hand and knee on the return — do not just drop them
- Keep your head in line with your spine, eyes looking at the floor

Sets/reps to start: 3 sets of 6 reps each side (6-8 second holds each)


=====================================================================
PART 2: HIP EXERCISES — MOBILITY AND STRENGTH
=====================================================================

Hip flexor tightness and glute weakness are among the most common
contributors to both sciatica and hip pain. These exercises directly
address your hip issues and support the core work above.


4. GLUTE BRIDGE
================

Starting position:
- Lie on your back
- Both knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart
- Arms at your sides, palms down
- Feet close enough that you can almost touch your heels with your fingertips

The movement:
- Squeeze your glutes and lift your hips until your body forms a straight
  line from knees to shoulders
- Hold at the top for 3-5 seconds
- Lower slowly back down

Key points:
- Drive through your heels, not your toes
- Do NOT arch your lower back at the top — stop when your hips are in line
- You should feel this in your glutes, not your lower back
- If you feel it in your hamstrings, move your feet slightly closer to you
- Breathe normally

Sets/reps: 3 sets of 10 reps (3-5 second hold at top)


5. CLAMSHELL
=============

Starting position:
- Lie on your side with knees bent at about 45 degrees
- Hips stacked on top of each other
- Head resting on your bottom arm
- Feet together

The movement:
- Keeping your feet together, raise your top knee as far as you can
  without letting your hips roll backward
- Hold for 2-3 seconds at the top
- Lower slowly
- Complete all reps on one side then switch

Key points:
- The movement is smaller than you think — if your hips roll, you went too far
- You should feel this in the side/back of your hip (gluteus medius)
- Keep your core braced so your torso stays still
- To increase difficulty, place a light resistance band just above your knees

Sets/reps: 3 sets of 12 reps each side


6. HIP FLEXOR STRETCH (HALF-KNEELING)
=======================================

Starting position:
- Kneel on one knee (put a pillow or towel under the knee for comfort)
- Other foot flat on the floor in front of you, knee at 90 degrees
- Torso upright, hands on your front knee or hips

The stretch:
- Gently shift your weight forward over your front foot
- You should feel a stretch in the front of your hip on the kneeling side
- Hold for 30 seconds
- Switch sides

Key points:
- Do NOT arch your lower back — tuck your pelvis slightly under (posterior tilt)
- The stretch should be moderate, not painful
- Squeeze the glute on the kneeling side to deepen the stretch
- Tight hip flexors pull on your lower back and contribute to sciatica

Sets: 2 holds of 30 seconds each side


7. SUPINE PIRIFORMIS STRETCH
==============================

This directly targets a muscle that, when tight, compresses the sciatic
nerve. If your sciatica runs through the back of your hip and down your
leg, this stretch is especially important for you.

Starting position:
- Lie on your back
- Both knees bent, feet flat on floor

The stretch:
- Cross your right ankle over your left knee (figure-4 position)
- Reach through and grab behind your left thigh (or use a towel if
  you can't reach)
- Gently pull your left thigh toward your chest
- You should feel a deep stretch in your right glute/hip
- Hold for 30 seconds
- Switch sides

Key points:
- Keep your head on the floor — do not strain your neck to reach
- Pull gently — this should feel like a deep stretch, not pain
- If you feel tingling or radiating pain, ease off
- Use a towel or belt looped behind the thigh if flexibility is limited

Sets: 2 holds of 30 seconds each side


=====================================================================
PART 3: BASIC RESISTANCE TRAINING — MUSCLE PRESERVATION
=====================================================================

At 57, in a caloric deficit, you will lose muscle unless you give your
body a reason to keep it. These are the simplest, spine-safe resistance
exercises to maintain muscle mass. All can be done at home with no
equipment (resistance bands optional).


8. WALL PUSH-UP
=================

Starting position:
- Stand facing a wall, arm's length away
- Place palms flat on the wall at shoulder height, shoulder-width apart
- Feet together or slightly apart

The movement:
- Bend your elbows and lean toward the wall in a controlled motion
- Touch (or nearly touch) your chest/nose to the wall
- Push back to the starting position
- Keep your body in a straight line throughout (no sagging hips)

Progression:
- Wall push-up (easiest) — start here
- Counter/table push-up (hands on a counter or sturdy table)
- Knee push-up (on the floor, from knees)
- Full push-up (only if spine tolerates and form is perfect)

Sets/reps: 3 sets of 10-15 reps


9. CHAIR-ASSISTED SQUAT
=========================

Starting position:
- Stand in front of a sturdy chair, facing away from it
- Feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly outward
- Arms extended in front of you for balance

The movement:
- Slowly lower yourself toward the chair as if sitting down
- Touch the chair lightly with your glutes (do not plop down)
- Stand back up by driving through your heels

Key points:
- Go only as low as your hips comfortably allow
- Keep your weight in your heels, not your toes
- Knees should track over your toes, not cave inward
- Keep your chest up and back neutral
- If this bothers your hips, reduce depth — even a quarter squat has value
- The chair is a safety net, not a seat

Progression:
- High chair / shallow squat — start here
- Standard chair, light touch
- No chair, bodyweight squat (when ready)

Sets/reps: 3 sets of 8-12 reps


10. RESISTANCE BAND ROW
=========================

Starting position:
- Sit in a chair with good posture
- Loop a resistance band around a doorknob or sturdy post at chest height
- Hold one end in each hand, arms extended in front of you
- There should be light tension in the band at the start

The movement:
- Pull the band toward your ribcage, squeezing your shoulder blades together
- Hold for 2 seconds at the back
- Slowly return to start

Key points:
- Keep your elbows close to your body
- Do not lean back — your torso stays still
- Focus on squeezing between your shoulder blades
- If no band available: use a towel looped around a doorknob and pull
  isometrically (hold tension for 10 seconds)

Sets/reps: 3 sets of 12-15 reps


=====================================================================
PROGRAMMING — PUTTING IT ALL TOGETHER
=====================================================================

DAILY SESSIONS (ALTERNATE BETWEEN TWO ROUTINES):

Routine A — Core + Hips (Mon, Wed, Fri, or every other day):
  1. Curl-up
  2. Side plank
  3. Bird-dog
  4. Glute bridge
  5. Clamshell
  6. Hip flexor stretch
  7. Piriformis stretch
  Time: 15-20 minutes

Routine B — Resistance (Tue, Thu, or 2-3x per week):
  1. Wall push-up
  2. Chair-assisted squat
  3. Resistance band row
  4. Glute bridge (doubles as resistance and hip work)
  Time: 10-15 minutes

You can also combine both routines into one 25-30 minute session if
you prefer fewer, longer sessions.


MCGILL BIG 3 REP SCHEME (descending pyramid):
  Set 1:  6 reps
  Set 2:  4 reps
  Set 3:  2 reps
  Hold each rep for 6-8 seconds.

ALL OTHER EXERCISES:
  3 sets of the prescribed reps
  Rest 30-60 seconds between sets

STRETCHES:
  2 holds of 30 seconds each side
  Do these at the end of the session when muscles are warm


PROGRESSION:

McGill Big 3:
  Week 1-2:   6/4/2 reps, 6 second holds
  Week 3-4:   8/6/4 reps, 8 second holds
  Week 5-6:   10/8/6 reps, 10 second holds
  Continue adding reps before increasing hold times.

Hip exercises:
  Week 1-2:   Glute bridges 3x8, clamshells 3x10
  Week 3-4:   Glute bridges 3x10, clamshells 3x12
  Week 5-6:   Glute bridges 3x12 (add 3 sec hold), clamshells 3x15 or add band

Resistance exercises:
  Week 1-2:   2 sets of each (not 3). Learn the form.
  Week 3-4:   3 sets of each at the low end of the rep range
  Week 5-6:   3 sets, increase reps or progress to harder variation
  Week 7+:    Progress variations (counter push-ups, deeper squats, heavier band)


WHAT TO AVOID
==============
- Sit-ups and crunches (repeated spinal flexion under load)
- Superman exercise (excessive spinal extension)
- Russian twists (rotational load on flexed spine)
- Heavy barbell work (excessive spinal compression)
- Any exercise that causes radiating pain down your leg
- Holding your breath during holds or reps
- Pushing through hip pain — reduce range of motion or skip that exercise


WHEN TO DO THEM
================
- Wait at least 30 minutes after waking (discs are most hydrated after
  sleep; give them time to settle)
- Can be done before or after your walk
- Stretches are best after activity when muscles are warm
- Do NOT do them when already fatigued or during a sciatica flare
  (see reference/flare-up-protocol.txt)
