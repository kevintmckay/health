DAILY SCHEDULE — EVERYTHING TIED TOGETHER
============================================

This is a realistic daily framework, not a rigid timetable. Adjust the
specific times to fit your life. What matters is the order and the habits.

IMPORTANT: You do not do all of this on day one. See the phased rollout
at the bottom of this document.


MORNING
========

Wake up (same time every day, including weekends)
  |
  v
Get outside for 10 minutes of daylight
  - Walk to the mailbox, sit on the porch, stand in the yard
  - This resets your circadian rhythm (see sleep plan)
  |
  v
Meditation (3-15 min depending on your current week)
  - Sit in a chair, breathing focus
  - Do this before you check your phone or email
  - See meditation plan for weekly progression
  |
  v
Breakfast
  - Protein-first meal from the diet plan
  - Eat at the table, not standing or in front of a screen
  - No decisions here — pick from your list and make it routine
  |
  v
Morning walk
  - Follow the walking plan for your current week's duration and days
  - Rest days are in the walking plan — on those days, skip this
  - Flat surface, comfortable pace
  - This is your main cardio for the day


MIDDAY
=======

Lunch
  - Protein + vegetables + small starch from the diet plan
  - Last caffeine of the day must be before this meal
  |
  v
Exercise session (on scheduled days — see programming below)
  - Routine A days: McGill Big 3 + hip exercises (15-20 min)
  - Routine B days: Resistance exercises (10-15 min)
  - See core-exercises.txt for all exercise details and progressions
  - Stretches (hip flexor + piriformis) go at the end when muscles are warm


AFTERNOON
==========

This is the highest risk window for emotional eating.
Most people hit an energy dip and an anxiety spike between 2-5 PM.

WEEKS 1-2: Just notice. If you eat outside a planned meal, log it in
your food/mood journal afterward: what you ate, what you were feeling,
what happened before. No pressure to change anything yet. You are
collecting data.

WEEKS 3-4: Start catching the thought. When you feel an urge, pause
and ask: "What thought just went through my head?" Write it down.
Still no pressure to resist the urge.

WEEKS 5+: Now start using the full toolkit:
  1. Pause. Do not go to the kitchen yet.
  2. Run the RAIN check (Recognize, Allow, Investigate, Non-identify)
  3. Ask: "Am I physically hungry or emotionally hungry?"
  4. If emotional: set a 15-minute timer, do something from your
     alternatives list (walk, water, call someone, small task)
  5. If physically hungry: have your planned snack (protein-based)
  6. Log it in your food/mood journal either way

Planned snack (if needed, any week)
  - Greek yogurt, protein shake, string cheese + turkey, hard boiled eggs
  - Eat it intentionally, sitting down


EVENING
========

Dinner
  - Protein + vegetables + small starch from the diet plan
  - Eat at the table
  - Stop eating when you are satisfied, not stuffed
  - No seconds for 10 minutes — if still hungry after 10, have more
    vegetables or protein
  |
  v
After dinner
  - If you have pool access, this is a good time for aquatic exercise
    (2x per week, see fitness plan)
  - Light activity is fine (easy walk, stretching, household tasks)
  - No intense exercise within 2 hours of bedtime
  |
  v
Kitchen closes
  - Pick a time (eg. 8:00 PM) and stop eating after that
  - Brush your teeth — this is a psychological signal that eating is done
  - If an urge hits after close: tea, water, or 4-7-8 breathing


BEFORE BED
===========

Wind-down routine starts 30-60 min before bedtime
  |
  v
  - Dim the lights, overhead lights off, lamps on
  - Screens off or night mode
  - No news, work email, or stressful content
  |
  v
15-30 min before bed:
  - Light stretching (hip flexor stretch and piriformis stretch are
    good here if you didn't do them earlier)
  - Read a book in a chair (not in bed — keep bed for sleep only)
  - Warm shower
  - Optional: short body scan meditation while seated
  |
  v
Lights out (same time every night)
  - Phone face down or in another room
  - Pillow under knees (back sleeping) or between knees (side sleeping)
  - If not asleep in 20 min, get up and sit in dim light until drowsy


CBT CHECK-IN (2 MINUTES, ANYTIME)
===================================

Once per day, write in your food/mood log. Can be at lunch, after
dinner, or before bed. Whatever you will actually do.

  Did I eat outside my planned meals today?   Y / N
  If yes:
    What was I feeling?    ___________
    What was the thought?  ___________
    What was the trigger?  ___________
  What went well today?    ___________

PHASED ROLLOUT — DO NOT SKIP THIS
====================================

WEEK 1:
  - Set consistent wake and sleep times
  - Morning daylight (10 min)
  - Walking per walking plan (4 days this week)
  - Start eating from the diet plan (ramp protein gradually)
  - Meditation: 3 minutes/day
  - Start food/mood log (just observe, no changes)

WEEK 2:
  - Continue all of the above
  - Walking per plan (4 days)
  - Meditation: 5 minutes/day
  - Kitchen closes rule starts

WEEK 3:
  - Add Routine A: McGill Big 3 + hip exercises (3x/week)
  - Walking per plan (5 days now)
  - CBT: start catching automatic thoughts
  - Meditation: 5 min + body scan

WEEK 4:
  - Add Routine B: resistance exercises (2x/week)
  - Walking per plan (5 days)
  - Meditation: 7 minutes/day

WEEK 5:
  - Add pool/aquatic exercise (2x/week) if available
  - CBT: start challenging and reframing thoughts
  - Meditation: 10 minutes/day

WEEK 6+:
  - Full routine as shown in the weekly view above
  - CBT: start urge surfing with the full afternoon protocol
  - Continue progressing walking, exercises, and meditation per their plans


ACCOUNTABILITY
================
Tell one person what you are doing. Research consistently shows this
improves long-term adherence. It does not need to be a big announcement.

Options:
  - A friend, partner, or family member
  - A walking partner (makes you show up on days you don't want to)
  - Your doctor or physical therapist
  - An online community (Reddit r/loseit, r/fitness, etc.)
  - Even just a daily text to someone: "walked 15 min today"

You do not need a coach or a gym membership. You just need one person
who knows you're doing this.


IF YOU GET DERAILED
=====================
- A bad day is not a reset. Do the next thing on the schedule.
- A bad week is not a failure. Pick back up where you left off.
- A sciatica flare is not the end — see flare-up-protocol.txt for
  what to keep doing and what to pause.
- A 70% day beats a 0% day every single time.
