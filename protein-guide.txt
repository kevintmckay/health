COMPLETE PROTEIN GUIDE
======================

DAILY TARGET: 120-140g protein per day

Based on research: 1.2-1.5 g/kg goal body weight for men 50+ during weight loss
(Research-supported range from meta-analyses, not the original 160g estimate)


PROTEIN SOURCES & PORTIONS
===========================

EGGS
----
Per serving: 1 egg = 6g protein
Daily limit: 3-4 eggs (18-24g)
Weekly limit: 21-28 eggs
Research: Up to 3-4 eggs/day safe based on meta-analyses
Uses: Breakfast, lentil sauté, snacks


CHICKEN THIGHS
--------------
Per serving: 4-5 oz cooked = 28-35g protein
Daily amount: 4-10 oz (at 1-2 meals)
Weekly amount: 50-70 oz (4-5 lbs raw)
Prep: Instant Pot 12 min, Air fryer 18-20 min at 400°F
Uses: Lentil sauté, dinner, batch prep Sunday


GREEK YOGURT (PLAIN, FULL-FAT)
-------------------------------
Per serving: 1 cup = 20g protein
Daily amount: 1-2 cups
Weekly amount: 7-14 cups (3-7 containers)
Uses: Breakfast, snacks


COTTAGE CHEESE (4% FULL-FAT)
-----------------------------
Per serving: 1 cup = 28g protein, 110 cal
Daily amount: 1-2 cups (unlimited, prioritize before bed)
Weekly amount: 7-14 cups (3-7 containers)
Why full-fat: More protein (28g vs 14g in fat-free), more satiating, only 30 cal difference
Uses: Before bed (slow casein protein prevents overnight muscle breakdown), breakfast alternative


TOFU (FIRM)
-----------
Per serving: 1/2 block (7-8 oz) = 20g total protein, 14g effective (70% bioavailability)
Daily amount: 1/2 to 1 block
Weekly amount: 2-4 blocks
Prep: Air fryer 400°F, 12-15 min (cubed)
Soy safety: Meta-analysis of 41 studies shows no effect on testosterone/estrogen in men
Uses: Lentil sauté 2-3x/week for variety


LENTILS (CANNED OR COOKED)
---------------------------
Per serving: 1 cup = 18g total protein, 10g effective (60% bioavailability)
Daily amount: 1-2 cups
Weekly amount: 7-14 cups (7 cans or 3 lbs dry)
Uses: Daily in lentil sauté, side dish, replaces starch


BLACK BEANS (CANNED)
---------------------
Per serving: 1 cup = 15g total protein, 10g effective
Daily amount: 1 cup (when not using lentils)
Weekly amount: 4-6 cans
Uses: Replace starch at meals, fiber source


GARBANZO BEANS/CHICKPEAS (CANNED)
----------------------------------
Per serving: 1 cup = 15g total protein, 10g effective
Daily amount: 1 cup (when not using lentils)
Weekly amount: 3-5 cans
Uses: Salads, emergency meals, variety


SALMON (FROZEN FILLETS)
------------------------

Weekly amount: 8-12 oz (2-3 servings)
Frequency: 2-3x per week (PRIORITY - anti-inflammatory)
Prep: Air fryer 400°F, 10-12 min
Research: 2-3 servings fatty fish/week provides sufficient omega-3 for inflammation reduction
Uses: Dinner, replace chicken 2-3x/week

CHEESE DAILY LIMIT (CRITICAL - EASY TO OVEREAT)
================================================
Solid cheese: 3 oz MAXIMUM per day (not including cottage cheese)
Example: 1 oz feta + 1 oz swiss + 1 string cheese = 3 oz total

Cottage cheese: UNLIMITED (1-2 cups/day recommended)

Pre-portion all cheese into 1 oz pieces when you get home from store
Never eat from the block

PROTEIN BIOAVAILABILITY REFERENCE
==================================

Animal proteins (90-95% absorbed):
- Eggs, chicken, fish, dairy: Count full amount

Plant proteins (lower absorption):
- Tofu: 70% absorbed (20g total = 14g effective)
- Lentils/beans: 60% absorbed (18g total = 10g effective)
- Pea protein: 85% absorbed (25g total = 21g effective)

This is why animal protein is prioritized, with plant protein as supporting role


RESEARCH SOURCES
================

Protein requirements for older adults:
- Meta-analysis: 1.2-1.5 g/kg body weight for muscle preservation during weight loss in adults 50+
- Enhanced protein (>1.3 g/kg/day) prevents muscle decline in overweight/obese adults

Egg consumption:
- 2020 meta-analysis (1.7M participants): Up to 1 egg/day not associated with CVD risk
- Moderate evidence supports 2-3 eggs/day as safe for most people
- ~33% of population are "hyper-responders" (need bloodwork to confirm)

Soy/testosterone:
- 2021 meta-analysis (41 studies, 1753 men): No effect on testosterone, estrogen, or sperm quality
- Safe up to 25g soy protein/day (well-studied)
